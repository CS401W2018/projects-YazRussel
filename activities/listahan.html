    4. Special entity characters usually starts with "&" and ends with ";". These are necessary to represent a character that are special functions for HTML which cannot be typed easily when coding. One of the example that Brother Slade gave during the discussion are:
    • &lt - this helps you produce or type "<" 
    • &nbsp
      • - this is the opposite of <br>
    • &gt
      • - this helps you produce or type ">"
    • &copy
      • - this helps you produce or type ©
  4. What is the difference between OL and UL tags?
    5. Though they have the same purpose of making list, they still have difference in use. <ul> is used for having a sequence of items that doesn't have to sort it out accordingly. While <ol> display a list in order to show the sequence, usually numerically.
