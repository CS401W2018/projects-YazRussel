Sinigang

Sinigang na Baboy is the ultimate comfort food! Made with pork ribs, vegetables, and tamarind-flavored broth, it's hearty and delicious on its own or served with steamed rice.

Ingredients:

  1. 2 pound pork spare ribs, cut into 2-inch pieces
  2. 8 cups of water
  3. 2 tomatoes, cut into quarter
  4. 1 onion, peeled and quartered
  5. 2 tablespoons of fish sauce (Patis)
  6. 6 pieces of taro (Gabi), peeled and cut in half
  7. 1 radish, peeled and slice to thick half-round
  8. 15 pieces of tamarind - preferrably the fresh pods, but you can use powder mixes which you can put 1 and a half
  9. 2 green finger chili
  10. Salt and pepper to taste
  11. Vegetables - feel free to add long beans, okra, bok choy, water spinach, and eggplant.

instructions:

  1. Rinse pork ribs and drain well.
  2. In a pot over medium heat, combine pork and enough water to cover. Bring to a boil, skimming scum that accumulates on top.
  3. Once broth clears, add tomatoes, onion, and fish sauce. Lower heat and simmer for about 1 to 1 Â½ hours or until meat is tender, adding more water as necessary to maintain about 8 cups.
  4. Add taro and cook for about 4 to 6 minutes or until tender.
  5. Add chili peppers and radish. Continue to simmer for about 2 to 3 minutes.
  6. Add long beans. Continue to cook for about 2 minutes.
  7. Add eggplant and okra and cook for another 1 to 2 minutes.
  8. If using packaged tamarind base, add to the pot and stir until completely dissolved.
  9. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  10. Add bok choy and continue to cook for about 1 minute. Serve hot.
